Enzyme progress curve
=====================

**Materials** 

* Tris-HCl stock buffer (1M, pH 8.5)
* Purified protease (with known concentrations in μg/mL or μM) 
* Fluorogenic peptide substrate stock solution (10 mM)
* DMSO
* 384 well black plate 
* Micropipette 
* Micropipette tips
* Microcentrifuge tube/PCR tube 

**Procedure**

*Assay buffer*

#. Add 0.5 mL of 1 M Tris-HCl stock buffer solution into 4.5 mL of distilled water to obtain 100 mM of Tris-HCl assay buffer solution at pH 8.5. Adjust the volume if necessary. 
#. Mix well. Vortex if necessary. 
#. Leave at bench for later use. 

*Protease dilution*

#. Retrive purified protease from -80 C and thaw at room temperature. 
#. Calculate volume of enzyme stock to be added into each well according to enzyme stock concentration. 
#. Add appropriate volume of enzyme into each well of a 384 well plate to the desired final enzyme concentration. 

    * Final enzyme concentration in wells: 20, 15, 10, 5 μM 

#. Top up each well with assay buffer until 36 μL. 

*Substrate serial dilution*

#. Prepare 7 microcentrifuge tubes. 
#. Add 30 μL of assay buffer into first tube. 
#. Add 20 μL of assay buffer into remaining tubes. 
#. Add 10 μL of substrate stock into first tube (Concentration of first tube = 2,500 μM @ 40 μL).
#. Transfer 20 μL of mixture from first tube to second tube, mix well by repeat pipetting. Transfer 20 μL of mixture from second tube to third tube, mix well by repeat pipetting. Repeat this step until the last tube. 

*In 384 well black plate*

#. Add 4 μL of substrate into corresponding wells. 

    * Optional: Split serial diluted substrate into smaller volume for each enzyme concentration before added into 384 well plate to prevent cross contamination.

#. Add 36 μL of assay buffer + 4 μL of substrate (no enzyme) into empty wells as blank. 
#. Read plate at 380 nm excitation and 445 nm emission every 5 mins for a total period of 9 hours. 

* This procedure demonstrated using 250 μM of substrate. 
* Repeat the whole procedure using various substrate concentration, i.e.: 125 μM, 62.5 μM, etc. All can be done in a single plate if planned accordingly.  

**Results**

* Plot RFU or product concentration (y-axis) against time (x-axis). 
* To find the Km and Vmax of the enzyme, plot reaction velocity against increasing concentration of substrate (enzyme concentration fixed).
